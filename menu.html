御節料理（おせちりょうり）

For Chinese New Year 2017 I decided I was going to make a exhaustive multi-course dinner for family and friends. Ironically I decided to make a traditional Japanese menu just to change it up from the usual Chinese cuisine. I had so much fun constructing a whole experience from scratch I decided to liberate my menu and give it back to you, the people. Cooking is an art, so refer to the amounts given for the ingredients as ratios instead of concrete amounts. Of course, if you do something different that turns out amazing, share it!

Not so fine print: Photos are representations only. My dishes did not always look like it did in the photos. I take no responsibility if the finished product is not camera ready. I also assume you know how to make steamed rice; prepare an abundance of it.

出汁 - Soup Base
煮干し昆布だし - Dried Infant Sardine and Kelp Stock
  • 1 L water
  • 10 g kombu (kelp)
  • 10 g niboshi (dried infant sardines)
  • 10 g katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes)

  • Note: The amount of kombu, niboshi, and katsuobushi should be 1% the amount of water.

Soak the kombu and niboshi in cold water for at least 30 minutes, up to overnight in the fridge.

Bring the water to a simmer on low heat, about 15 minutes, but don't let it boil. Take out the kombu and niboshi right before it boils and add the katsuobushi. Bring to a boil on medium heat, then turn off immediately. Let sit 10 minutes.

Strain over fine mesh, pressing out the dashi as it drips through.

鴨だし - Duck Stock
  • water
  • 1 whole duck
  • 1 welsh or spring onion
  • 1 ginger root

  • Note: This part requires you to butcher a duck. If you're not comfortable doing it, have your butcher quarter it for you.

  • Reference video.

Remove the wishbone from the collar of the duck. Remove the legs and breasts, leaving the wings on the duck. Carve the legs, removing the meat from the bones. Trim the breasts, legs, and caracass and save any duck fat you remove.

Place the onions, ginger, carcass, neck, gizzards, and liver in a pot and cover with water. Place the pot offset from the center of the flame and bring to a boil then reduce heat to lowest setting, skimming off any scum as it cooks.

The longer the stock cooks, the richer it will taste. I let mine stew overnight for a total of 24 hours, and the flavor was almost too strong. 6 to 8 hours might be a good compromise.

祝い肴 - Celebration Dishes
黒豆 - Boiled Black Soybeans
  • water
  • 400 g black soybeans
  • 200 g sugar
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp salt

Rinse the beans and wash gently. Let them soak overnight in water 1.5 to 2 times their height in a pot.

Next morning, add the rest of the ingredients, mix, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low and skim off the scum. Simmer beans for 2 hours, adjusting cooking time according to firmness of the beans. For faster cook time, use a pressure cooker, which will reduce cooking time by at least half.

When cooked, add sugar to taste. Top with gold flakes if you're feeling pompous.

田作り - Soy Sauce Candied Dried Baby Sardines
  • 50-100 g niboshi
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil

In a frying pan on low heat, toast the niboshi with sesame seeds until crispy.

Combine the sake, soy sauce, water, oil, and honey in a saucepan and put it over low heat until the mixture is thick.

Put the niboshi and sesame into the saucepan and coat with honey mixture. Spread out on a plate or pan and let cool.

数の子 - Marinated Herring Roe
  • 1 box kazunoko (herring roe)
  • 240 ml dashi
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 5 grams katsuobushi

  • Note: If you didn't make dashi you can buy Ajinomoto from most Asian markets.

Combine the dashi, sake, mirin, and soy sauce in a saucepan and bring to a simmer. Take off heat and let cool.

If the kazunoko still has its coating, remove it now. Place it in a container with the cooled marinade and let sit for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

When ready, remove the kazunoko from the marinade, and sprinkle with katsuobushi.

伊達巻 - Sweet Egg Roll
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 bag hanpen (soft fish cake)
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 1 tsp soy sauce
  • 1 sushi mat

Preheat oven to 200C.

Cut the hanpen into small squares, then throw everything into a blender. Blitz till it's smoother than a baby's butt.

Strain the mixture through a fine mesh, twice for best results. Line a shallow square baking sheet with parchment paper and pour in mixture.

Bake for 15 minutes at 200C, then 2 minutes at 180C. Remove from oven then place face down on sushi mat. Tightly roll up the eggs and secure the mat with rubber bands. Place in fridge for 30 minutes to 1 hour.

Liberate the egg roll and slice.

栗金団 - Chesnut and Sweet Potato Mash
  • 1 jar kuri kanroni (chesnuts in syrup)
  • 500 g satsumaimo (Japanese sweet potatoes)
  • 180 ml mirin
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 pinch saffron
  • 1 pinch salt

  • Note: The saffron is for coloring. Traditional recipes call for gardenia pod, but I couldn't find any.

Peel the sweet potatoes and cut them into 1 cm rounds. Boil them with the salt and saffron until they are mashable. Drain and let sit for 30 minutes.

In a heavy bottom pan, mix the sweet potatoes, mirin, honey, and the syrup from the kuri kanroni over low heat. Mix well until fragrant, then turn off the heat and work in chesnuts.

酢の物 - Vinegared Dishes
紅白なます - Daikon & Carrot Salad
  • 10 cm daikon
  • 5 cm carrot
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • 3 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 small piece of kombu

Peel the daikon and carrot, then julienne. Sprinkle with half the salt and massage. Let sit.

Combine kombu, water, sugar, vinegar, and rest of salt in a container or a ZipLock bag.

Squeeze water out of daikon and carrots and toss in with the marinade. Pickle for 30 minutes to 1 hour, remove from the marinade and serve.

菊蕪 - Chrysanthemum Turnip
  • 6 small turnips or 1 large turnip
  • 1 or 2 small pieces kombu
  • 100 ml rice vinegar
  • 3 tbsp sugar
  • salt

Peel turnips and place between two chopsticks. Slice in 1 mm increments to the chopstick, then turn 90 degrees and do the same.

Refernce video.

Mix kombu, vinegar, sugar, and pinch of salt in container or bag, add turnips, and pickle for 2 hours to overnight.

煮物 - Simmered Dishes
煮しめ - Simmered Vegetables
  • 1 block konnyaku
  • 1 bamboo shoot
  • 1 section lotus root
  • 1/2 section gobo (burdock root)
  • 5 satoimo (baby taro)
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 pack shimeji mushrooms or 6 shiitake mushrooms
  • 500 ml dashi
  • 3 tbsp sake
  • 3 tbsp mirin
  • 3 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tsp salt
  • rice vinegar

Peel the gobo and carrot then cut them into trapezoidal pieces. Soak the gobo in a 10% rice vinegar solution. Peel the bamboo and lotus and slice them thin. Soak sliced lotus in 10% rice vinegar solution. Peel the satoimo and cut them in half. Rub with salt and rinse. Separate the lower part of the stem on the shimeji mushrooms and discard.

Rip block of konnyaku into bite sized pieces by hand and then boil off the excess smell. Drain and set aside. In a pan sauté all ingredients in some oil, then add the dashi and bring to simmer. Add the soy sauce, mirin, sake, and salt. Cover with false lid and cook for 10 minutes on high. Remove false lid and cook 10 minutes more. Adjust seasoning to taste.

クリームシチュー - Cream Stew
  • 2 portions boneless duck leg meat
  • 50 grams thick cut bacon
  • 2 medium onions
  • 2 medium carrots
  • 2 medium potatoes
  • 1 small broccoli
  • 50 g butter
  • 50 g flour
  • 500 ml milk
  • 100 ml cream
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 tsp nutmeg
  • 1,000 ml duck stock
  • salt and pepper

Cut onions into wedges, slice carrots, and cut up the potatoes. Cut the broccoli florets and peel off the thick parts of the skin. Cut duck leg meat into squares, and save the fat.

Make the roux. Melt the butter over low heat, then stir in the flour. Stir until you get a wet sand texture. Add the milk bit by bit until it is all stirred in, with no lumps in the roux.

sauté bacon in saucepan over medium heat until lightly browned. Add duck leg meat and some duck fat. sauté until duck changes color, then remove all from pan. Discard the fat. Add onions to pan, sauté, then add carrots. Once the vegetables have some color on them, add the dashi and bay leaf and bring to boil. Lower heat and simmer for 10 mins. Add potatoes, broccoli, duck, and bacon and simmer until vegetables are tender.

Make the roux. Melt the butter over low heat, then stir in the flour. Stir until you get a wet sand texture. Add the milk bit by bit until it is all stirred in, with no lumps in the roux.

Set aside half of the stock, and combine the roux with the ingredients. Add back some of the stock if stew is too thick. Add the nutmeg and cream, then salt and pepper to taste. Serve over rice.

鯖の味噌煮 - Norwegian Mackerel Simmered in Miso
  • 1 saba
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 3 tbsp red miso
  • 1 tbsp white miso
  • 200 ml sake
  • 100 ml water
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 3 slices ginger

  • Note: You will have to filet a saba. here is a tutorial.

Fillet the fish and remove the bones. Cut the filets in half and score the skin in an X shape. Mix the mirin and the red and white miso into a paste.

Combine the sake, water, and sugar in a shallow pan and bring to a boil. Add the saba fillets skin side up and cover with false lid. Cook for 3 minutes. Add in 2/3 of the miso paste and the ginger. Cover and simmer for 5 minutes. Then add the rest of the paste and simmer till sauce thickens, basting the saba as you cook.

焼き肴
ハマチのカマの塩焼き - Grilled Yellowtail Collar
  • 1 hamachi kama (yellowtail collar)
  • salt

If you caught a yellowtail and are wondering which part of the fish the collar is, it's the part right behind the gills, stretching from the top of the head to the belly. The collar and the cheek are the best parts of any fish.

Preheat oven to 200C. Salt collar liberally. Place skin side down and bake until top is golden. Cooking times vary.

鯖の塩焼き - Grilled Norwegian Mackerel
  • 1 whole saba
  • salt

Fillet the saba as shown above. Hoever, this time you want to score the skin all the way up and down the fillet instead of making one X.

Preheat oven to 200C. Salt the saba liberally. Place the fillets skin side down and bake for 15 minutes.

汁物 - Soups
雑煮 - Grilled Mochi in Duck Base Soup
  • dashi
  • duck stock
  • 1 slab kamaboko (firm fish cake)
  • hard mochi (store bought)
  • 2 duck breasts
  • 2 welsh onions
  • shichimi (japanese pepper mix)
  • 1 bunch mitsuba (honewort)
  • trimmings from duck

Cut welsh onions into 1 inch segments. Discard the leaves. Slice the kamaboko into thin slices.

Combine the duck sock and dashi into one pot, and add in the sliced kamaboko. Set over low heat. Salt the duck breast then sear in a pan skin side down until golden brown, then flip over and repeat. Sear till duck breast is medium rare. Remove the duck breasts from pan, and slice. Render the duck fat with the fat left over from the breasts, then fry the welsh onions.

Set toaster oven to 200C, then put in hard blocks of mochi. When the mochi rises and browns slightly, it is ready. Place mochi, welsh onion, kamaboko, and duck breast slice in bowl. Fill bowl with duck soup and place a leaf of mitsuba on top. Sprinkle shichimi if desired.

年越し蕎麦 - Toshikoshi Soba
  • soba (store bought)
  • quail eggs
  • leftovers
  • shichimi

This is a great way to clean up various leftovers from New Years. Bring a pot of salted water to boil, and throw in the soba. When done, which is after about 1 minute, strain the soba and rinse with cold water.

Use the duck soup from the zoni and put everything in a bowl. Crack a quail egg over the noodle and mix. Sprinkle shichimi if desired.

デザート - Dessert
大福
  • 115 g Mochiko (sweet rice flour)
  • 180 ml water
  • 50 g granulated sugar
  • 100 g potato starch
  • 200 g anko (red bean paste)
  • 100 g kuromame

Combine Mochiko and sugar in bowl and mix, then add water and mix until combined.

Cover loosely with cling wrap and microwave on high for 1 minute. Remove then mix with wet spatula. Repeat until mixture becomes translucent and firms up.

Cover a baking sheet or counter with parchment paper and dust with potato starch. Pour mochi onto parchment and divide up the mochi into 8-10 rounds. Roll out the mochi until thin. Remember to use plenty of potato starch on hands and cooking tools.

Place a few kuromame onto mochi and then fold it, then spread it out. Place a small ball of anko into middle of mochi, then wrap it up, stretching and pulling the mochi and pinching it to close.

Remove excess starch from the surface and form the daifuku into a round, even shape. Place it on a tray dusted with starch, and cover with foil, to prevent drying out.

抹茶とホワイトチョコのパウンドケーキ - Matcha White Chocolate Loaf Cake
  • 3 eggs
  • softened butter - same weight as eggs
  • caster sugar - same weight as eggs
  • all-purpose flour - same weight as eggs
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp matcha (green tea powder)
  • 70 g white chocolate chips

Preheat oven to 170C, and line a medium sized loaf pan (7.5in x 7.5in x 3.4in) with parchment paper. Weight eggs, then weigh out same amount of butter, sugar, and flour.

Beat sugar and butter together with mixer until fluffy. Mix in eggs one at a time, mixing well before adding each egg. Sift in flour, baking powder, and matcha. Combine using spatula. Stir in white chocolate chips.

Pour batter into loaf pan, bake in oven for 40 minutes.

ハッピーセット - Happy Meal
となりのトトロ おにぎり
  • steamed white rice
  • black sesame seeds
  • nori (toasted seaweed)
  • salt
  • quail eggs
  • sliced white cheese

Onigri is hard to explain and much easier to learn visually. I will forgive you if you decide to follow the instructions either here or here. However, don't forget the quail egg umbrella!

Grind the black sesame in a mortar and combine with rice. Wet your hands and in the palm of one hand place a pinch of salt. Take some rice with your other hand and shape it into a ball. Then shape it into a triangle with neat round corners.

Shape two small ears and set aside. Cut a piece of sliced cheese in a semicircle, or shape some white rice in an oval, and place on front of onigri.

Cut some nori into 2 circular pieces, 6 thin long pieces, and 8 triangular pieces, all small sized. Cut 2 circular pieces of cheese. Place these according to a portrait of Totoro. Stick on the ears.

Fry a quail egg sunny side up, sprinkle with a pinch of salt, and place on top of Totoro's head as his umbrella.

たこさんウィンナー - Octopus Shaped Red Wieners
  • 1 pack Japanese red wieners

This one is simple. Cut the wiener all the way through leaving about a quarter of the sausage uncut. Then roll the wiener 90 degrees and do the same. Rotate 45 degrees for one more cut.

Toss the wieners in a skillet and sauté till the tentacles curl.
